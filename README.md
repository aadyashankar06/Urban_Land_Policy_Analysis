# Urban Land Policy Analysis – India

## Overview
This project analyzes India's urban land inefficiency problem, estimated at ₹40 lakh crore worth of idle land, and proposes a scalable redevelopment framework to unlock economic value and improve housing supply.

## Problem Statement
- Severe urban housing shortage in India  
- Large amounts of underutilized public land  
- Regulatory, legal, and financial barriers prevent redevelopment  
- Rising urban population with limited affordable housing supply  

## Key Insights
- ~47,000 hectares of railway land lies idle in urban corridors  
- Over 95% of housing demand comes from EWS/LIG segments  
- Urban population expected to reach ~53% by 2047  
- Affordable housing supply has declined significantly in recent years  

## Approach
- Identified three key failure areas:
  - Regulatory & Legal Failures  
  - Environmental Constraints  
  - Financial Barriers  

- Designed a **3-tier institutional framework (NBRM)**:
  - National level authority  
  - State-level execution bodies  
  - City-level implementation units  

- Proposed key policy mechanisms:
  - Single Window Clearance System (SWCS)  
  - Transferable Development Rights (TDR)  
  - Stacked Floor Space Index (FSI) model  

## Impact
- Unlocks ₹40 lakh crore economic value  
- Boosts affordable housing supply  
- Mobilizes private capital with minimal government expenditure  
- Enables faster and scalable urban redevelopment
